Waste Management

Waste management is the process of collecting, segregating, treating, recycling, and disposing of waste in an environmentally responsible manner.

Types of Waste:
1. Wet Waste:
- Food scraps
- Vegetable peels
- Garden waste
- Organic matter

Management of Wet Waste:
- Segregate at source.
- Use compost bins.
- Convert organic waste into manure.
- Avoid mixing with dry waste.

2. Dry Waste:
- Paper
- Plastic
- Glass
- Metal

Management of Dry Waste:
- Recycle reusable materials.
- Separate paper, plastic, and metal.
- Send recyclable waste to authorized recycling centers.

3. E-Waste:
- Mobile phones
- Computers
- Batteries
- Electronic devices

Management of E-Waste:
- Dispose through certified e-waste collection centers.
- Never burn electronic waste.

Benefits of Proper Waste Management:
- Reduces environmental pollution.
- Improves public health.
- Conserves natural resources.
- Supports sustainable development.
