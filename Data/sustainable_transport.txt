Sustainable Transportation

Sustainable transportation refers to transport systems that minimize environmental impact while meeting the mobility needs of people.

Objectives of Sustainable Transportation:
- Reduce carbon emissions.
- Improve air quality.
- Reduce traffic congestion.
- Promote energy-efficient travel.

Modes of Sustainable Transportation:

1. Public Transportation
- Buses
- Metro systems
- Local trains

Benefits:
- Reduces fuel consumption.
- Lowers greenhouse gas emissions.
- Decreases road congestion.

2. Cycling
- Environment-friendly.
- Improves physical health.
- Requires no fossil fuels.

3. Walking
- Zero carbon emissions.
- Promotes healthy lifestyles.
- Reduces traffic congestion.

4. Electric Vehicles (EVs)
- Lower emissions compared to conventional vehicles.
- Energy efficient.
- Support cleaner transportation systems.

Sustainable Transportation on Campuses:
- Encourage bicycle usage.
- Provide dedicated cycling tracks.
- Promote carpooling among students and staff.
- Use electric campus shuttle services.
- Improve pedestrian-friendly infrastructure.

Benefits of Sustainable Transportation:
- Reduced air pollution.
- Lower carbon footprint.
- Improved public health.
- Reduced fuel consumption.
- Support for sustainable cities and communities.

SDG Alignment:
SDG 11 – Sustainable Cities and Communities
SDG 13 – Climate Action

Role of Students:
- Prefer walking or cycling for short distances.
- Use public transportation whenever possible.
- Participate in carpooling initiatives.
- Support environmentally friendly transportation practices.
